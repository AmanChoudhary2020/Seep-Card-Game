Seep Card Game

How To Play

Quick Requisites

  • The value of a card is determined by the face value of the card (eg., 3 of hearts = 3, 10 of clubs = 10)
  • Special Cases: Jack = 11, Queen = 12, King = 13, Ace = 1

Setup

  • Decide who goes first; first player can only see 4/8 of the cards in their hand on first turn
  • An additional four cards placed faced down in center of table

Step One: The Deal and Bid

  • The first player declares bid: a value ≥ to 9 that matches the value of one of the cards in that player's hand
    • Example: player one declares bid of 12 because Queen of Spades in player one's hand
  • If player one doesn't have a card with a value ≥ to 9 in hand, re-draw hand and repeat
  • Once player one has declared a bid, the four cards on the table are revealed
    • NOTE: the other four cards in player one's hands are still hidden (they will be revealed once player one's turn is finished)

Step Two: The First Play

  • From there, player 1 has three choices:
    • Option 1: Create a House
    • Option 2: Pick Up
    • Option 3: Throw Loose Card

Creating a House
  • Requirements to create a house:
    • Houses can only have values ≥ 9
    • To create a house of a certain value, you must have a card of that value in your hand
      • Ex: A player can only build a house of value 13 if there is a King (any suit) in that player's hand
  • Method #1: Combining a card from your hand with an existing card on table to create a house
    • Ex: Adding a three of clubs from your hand to a six of diamonds on the table to create a house of value 9
  • Method #2: Combining a card from player hand with two or more existing cards on table to create a house
    • Ex: Combining a three of clubs and five of diamonds from the table, with an ace of hearts from player hand to create a house of value 9

Pick Up
  • Requirements to pickup:
    • Players can pick up cards of any value!
    • To pickup, player places a card with matching value ....
  • #1: Picking up non-houses
    • Combining a card of a certain value from player hand with one existing card on table with same value, to pick up
      • Ex: Picking up a 5 of clubs on the table with a 5 of diamonds from player hand
    • Combining a card of a certain value from player hand with two or more existing cards on table with same value, to pick up
      • Ex: Combining a 3 of hearts and 2 of spades on the table, and picking them up with a 5 of diamonds from player hand
  • #2: Picking up houses
    • Ex: Combining a three of clubs and five of diamonds from the table, with an ace of hearts from player hand to create a house of value 9

Throw Loose Card
  • Requirements to pickup:
    • Players can pick up cards of any value!
    • To pickup, player places a card with matching value ....
  • #1: Picking up non-houses
    • Combining a card of a certain value from player hand with one existing card on table with same value, to pick up
      • Ex: Picking up a 5 of clubs on the table with a 5 of diamonds from player hand
    • Combining a card of a certain value from player hand with two or more existing cards on table with same value, to pick up
      • Ex: Combining a 3 of hearts and 2 of spades on the table, and picking them up with a 5 of diamonds from player hand
  • #2: Picking up houses
    • Ex: Combining a three of clubs and five of diamonds from the table, with an ace of hearts from player hand to create a house of value 9
